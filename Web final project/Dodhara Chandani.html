Dodhara Chandani is the longest multi-span pedestrian suspension bridge in Nepal. This bridge is over the Mahakali River or the Sarda River. Similarly, this bridge lies in the Bhimdatta Municipality in the Kanchanpur District of Nepal. Its official name is Dodhara Chandani Multispan Pedestrian Bridge. Moreover, the Dodhara Chandani Bridge’s length is 1452.96 m. The bridge is an incredible example of Nepalese Engineering. Dodhara Chandani Bridge connects two villages Dodhara and Chandani. These two villages are separated by the Mahakali River. Likewise, this bridge is famous for the other name Mahakali Bridge. The bridge is very beautiful to see. But the motorcycles crossing from the bridge annoy the visitors a little bit. This bridge would be better if they would only allow pedestrians to cross and visit across. Mostly, visitors enjoy sunset views from this bridge.

This bridge is famous for its unique engineering technique. Moreover, this bridge is near Shuklaphanta National Park. Most of the visitors who visit the national park do not miss visiting this unique bridge. The famous Dodhara Chandani Bridge was built in the year 2005. This Pedestrian Bridge has a significant impact on the local communities and local people in the area. This structure has a great impact, particularly on those living in the Dodhara and Chandani villages. The suspension bridge provides a safe way crossing for school children, on the other side of the river. Moreover, the long suspension bridge has opened up new opportunities for small businesses. It has been very beneficial for people who sell goods at local markets. Before the construction of the bridge, the local people have to depend upon the vendors to cross the river which was slow and expensive.

Architecture of Dodhara Chandani Bridge

Dodhara Chandai Suspension Bridge lies in the far west of Nepal. Moreover, the Dodhara Chandani Bridge is 1452.96 m long. It is a type of Multi Span Pedestrian Suspension Bridge. This bridge has the support of eight pylons (pillars) in the middle of the bridge. Likewise, visitors can see the bridge slightly curved upwards in the middle of two pylons. The bridge mostly consists of a steel structure. Although the bridge is designed for pedestrians only, you can find many bicycles and motorcycles crossing the bridge as well. The nearest place to this bridge is Mahendranagar Bazar. If you reach Mahendranagar, you can easily reach this beautiful and long suspension bridge. Steel Cables support the weight of the bridge and are anchored to the ground on either side of the river. Meanwhile, the cables are connected to large concrete towers which support the weight of the whole bridge.

The bridge is a unique architecture of Engineering. This bridge not only serves as a means of transportation but also provides a beautiful viewpoint for visitors who visit the far western region of Nepal. Steel cables from the pillar towers suspend the long bridge. This provides a safe and convenient crossing for pedestrians and cyclists. Before the construction of the bridge, the people of the other side villages traveled a long way to reach Mahendranagar. They had to travel all to Khatima in India and cross the Sarada Barrage there. Finally, they could reach Mahendranagar, which was the place for the market and it was the district headquarter as well. The water level is very high during the monsoon seasons.

How to reach Dodhara Chandani Bridge

Dodhara Chandani Bridge lies in Bhimdatta Municipality in the Kanchanpur District of Nepal. Similarly, it is very near Mahendranagar. Mahendranagar, currently known as Bhimdattanagar lies at a distance of approximately 705 km from Kathmandu, Nepal. Dodhara Chandani Bridge is at a distance of 10 km from Bhimdattanagar. It may take around 18 hours to reach Bhimdattanagar from Kathmandu, Nepal. From Bhimdattanagar, you can reach Dodhara Chandani Bridge in around 20 minutes.

What is the history of Dodhara Chandani Temple?
