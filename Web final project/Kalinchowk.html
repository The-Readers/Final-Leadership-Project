Kalinchowk is a popular tourist destination located in the Dolakha district of the eastern hilly region of Nepal. The name comes from two words, Kali and Chowk. The first word “Kali” is a “Hindu goddess”, and the second word, "Chowk”, means “a flat area”. Thus, Kalinchowk means a flat area where the Hindu goddess Kali is worshipped. This place is famous for Kalinchowk Bhagwati Temple and Kuri Village. Kalinchowk Bhagwati Temple is a Hindu shrine. Moreover, the beautiful place is known for its natural beauty, trekking trails, famous Kalinchowk Bhagwati Temple, Kuri village, and snowfall.


Certainly, Kalinchowk is one of the most visited tourist destinations in winter. Moreover, this place offers a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains, which include Mt. Everest, Kanchenjunga, and Gaurishankar. Moreover, the landscape view of the hills below is one of the most beautiful scenes from the temple area. We can see the visitor enjoying snowfall during the winter season. Similarly, this place gets snowfall mainly at the end of December. And it lasts till the end of February or mid-March most often. During the time of snowfall, it is difficult for jeeps, buses, and cars to reach the top of Kuri Village. Taking your vehicle or motorbike to the Kuri village is one of the adventures here.

Why is Kalinchowk the destination for tourism?

Kalinchowk is located at an altitude of 3842 meters from sea level. Likewise, you can see stunning views of mountain ranges, including >Mt. Gaurishankar, Mt. Jugal, Mt. Kanchenjunga, Mt. Annapurna Range, and Mt Everest. The forest area around this place offers tourists multi-colour Rhododendrons, birds, and animals. Similarly, the region is located in the Gaurishankar Conservation Area where two famous rivers of Nepal, Sunkoshi and Tamakoshi, originate.

Kalinchowk is also famous for trekking. Trekkers can drive up to Charikot and stay overnight, exploring the local lifestyle, culture, customs, and traditions. On the next early morning, after having breakfast, trekkers can trek to Kuri Village with a lot of adventure. Moreover, during the trek from Charikot to Kuri Village, trekkers will have stunning views of the Himalayas, monasteries, and different birds and animals. After reaching Kuri Village, trekkers can take rest and stay overnight in hotels. The next day early in the morning 1.5-hour trek or 5 mins cable car drive will take you to Kalinchowk Bhagwati Temple. You can also see the Kathmandu Valley from the temple if the weather is clear.

Visitors visiting this beautiful area will have a lot of things to experience and explore. Among the various events and places to explore and experience, enjoying the snowfall during the winter season is a major event. Kalinchowk Bhagwati Temple

The Kalinchowk Bhagwati Temple above Kuri Village is a major attraction for tourists. The temple lies at the top of the hill above Kuri Village. The temple dedicates to the Hindu goddess Kali. And it is one of the most sacred places in Nepal. The temple is at the hilltop, offering a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains, including Mt Everest, Kanchenjunga, and Gaurishankar. Thousands of pilgrims visit this holy temple every year, especially during the Kali Puja festival in October or November. At the time of the festival, the temple is decorated with lights and flowers. Likewise, people from all over Nepal and abroad also visit the temple to offer prayers to the goddess and seek blessings.

More about Kalinchowk

Kalinchowk is also popular for its traditional culture and local lifestyle. Most of the people in the region are Hindus and rich in cultural heritage. Similarly, tourists enjoy the traditional dress of the local people. Similarly, the local cuisine made by the local people out there is delicious. Tourists visiting here mostly visit nearby attractions such as the Dolakha Bhimsen Temple, dedicated to the Hindu deity Lord Bhimsen. The temple is located in the town of Dolakha, which is about 5 km away from Charikot Bazar. The Dolakha Bhimsen Temple is considered to be one of the most important and sacred pilgrimage sites for Hindus in Nepal.

Most of the visitors visiting this place go for Kalinchowk Bhagwati Darsan and to enjoy snowfall. Meanwhile, we can find tourists taking vehicles up to Charikot on the first day. On the second day, the visitors seem to be trekking up to the Kuri Village and Kalinchowk Bhagwati Temple. Similarly, trekking up to the hilltop helps you to experience the natural beauty. Likewise, you can experience different kinds of birds on the way and may encounter wildlife if you are lucky enough.
