Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple is a Hindu Temple that lies in Telkot, Changunarayan Municipality, Bhaktapur. This temple is dedicated to Goddess Laxmi. In the Hindu religion, Goddess Laxmi is worshipped as a Goddess of Wealth. This temple is very famous for the Hindu religion. Moreover, the name Pancha Maha Laxmi means Five Big Lords Laxmi Temple. This beautiful temple is on the way to Nagarkot just 2 km from Telkot towards Sankhu village. You can reach the temple by Sakhu-Sali Nadi Temple- Nagarkot Road or Bhaktapur-Telkot-Nagarkot Road. Besides, this temple lies on the lap of Nagarkot Forest. The temple lies on the foot of green hills in a peaceful environment. In addition, the interior of the temple is amazing to watch. Devotees visiting this place can listen to Bhagwat Gita in the main temple.

This temple consists of all five Laxmi Goddesses. Likewise, the temple is built in a modern style with many facilities in the temple. The main temple has a big hall inside where you can find devotees singing and chanting the Bhajan of Goddess Laxmi, other gods, and goddesses. You can find devotees doing meditation inside the main temple and listening to the spiritual saying of Gurus. The architecture of the temple is really amazing to watch. Similarly, there is a Gurukul facility for students to read the Sanskrit Language and Dharma Grantha. The modern-style temple just above the main Pancha Laxmi Temple is the center of attraction of the place. Likewise, this place remains crowded during weekends and public holidays. People visiting Nagarkot or Sali Nadi Temple are strongly recommended to visit this place. Similarly, this temple is home to the Ashram of Swami Kamalnayanacharya Shree Ji Maharaj.

About Swami Kamalnayan Acharya Shri Ji Maharaj

Swami Kamalnayan Acharya Shri Ji Maharaj is a Hindu spiritual leader. He origins from Nepal. The main saying of Swami Kamalnayan is “World free of destructive weapons and drug addiction”. He is working on this mission by providing spiritual ideas in different parts of Nepal and the world. Furthermore, Swami is working with different personalities, and national and international institutions for the betterment of the whole world. He was very enthusiastic and curious about spiritual ideas and religious talks from childhood. Swami was fond of nature and different animals since childhood. He completed his elementary education in the local town and went to Banaras, India for further education.

Swami Kamalnayan Acharya Shri Ji Maharaj is the man behind the establishment of Akhanda Jyoti Baba Swami Shreeji Charitable Trust. This trust has done renowned works all over Nepal. This trust has made betterments in providing accessibility to various pilgrimage sites in the country. Moreover, this trust is famous for the establishment of Bishramsthal(Rest House), Dharmashala, and Aananda Griha all over the country. This trust runs a Gurukul school named Shree Muktinath Veda Vidyashram. Likewise, this school provides free food, lodge, and education in Vedic scriptures. This includes Grammar, English, Mathematics, Literature, and Music. You can find these established in the villages of Kudule, Phedi, and Baglung in Nepal. Swami Kamalnayan is awarded the titles of Muktinath Peethadhish, Ananta Shree Vibhushith, and Akhandajyoti Baba Anantashree.

Architecture and Features


Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple and Hayagrib Cave at Bhaktapur at the top of Baglung is a beautiful temple complex. Similarly, the architecture of the temple is in the traditional Nepali style of architecture as well as the South Indian Structure of Temples. The temple complex has several temples that include the main temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is beautified with carvings and sculptures. Likewise, we can find interior decoration with colorful murals and paintings. In addition to the main temple, there are several smaller temples that are dedicated to other Hindu deities. Other Hindu deities are Lord Ganesha, Lord Krishna and Radha, and Goddess Durga. There is a garden at the top of the temple complex. At the end of the garden, there is an eye-catching big statue of Lord Hanuman holding a Sumeru Parbat in his left hand.

The design of the temple complex is in such a way that it creates a peaceful atmosphere. Tourists come here not only to visit but to worship and meditate also. The complex lies at the hilltop above Baglung Bazaar amongst dense green forests and hills. The architecture and style of the temple complex add beauty to the place. Similarly, the architecture makes it a popular tourist destination for religious significance as well. There is a belief that visiting the temple complex and offering prayers can bring blessings and good fortune. According to the locals, you can find tigers in the temple complex sometimes.

Places to visit around Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple

The temple complex situated at the base of Nagarkot Hill has many things to offer visitors. The Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple complex has many attractions around the temple area. The Hayagrib Cave just above the temple is one of the attractions of the place. Similarly, there is a Glass Temple called Seesh Mahal behind the main Shree Pancha Laxmi Temple. There is a cafeteria or shop near the cave where you can have food and drinks. The surrounding of the temple is really peaceful. There are gardens with different kinds of flowers. Moreover, you can find many groups of people enjoying picnics in the area.

Hayagrib Cave

This cave is near the way gate of Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple. The cave is attractive and beautifully decorated with paintings and statues at the entrance of the cave. There is a gate with a big tiger statue at the entrance. To enter the cave, you need to get a ticket at the counter. Besides, People made the cave artificially. The path inside the cave remains slippery during rainy days. The cave is approximately 50 meters long that takes you to a Temple like a small building. This building has many statues of Gods and Goddesses. While returning from the cave, you need to take the way below through the jungle. The path while returning passes through the dense green forest. This cave is famous among content creators on the internet.

Sheesh Mahal

There is a place behind the main Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple called Sheesh Mahal. Basically, this is a temple with glass structures and decorations on the walls and ceilings of the room. There is a statue of Goddess Laxmi in the middle of the Mahal. You can see the beautifully decorated statue with flowers and clothes. You need to take your shoes off before entering the Sheesh Mahal. To sum up, you should not miss Sheesh Mahal if you are visiting Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple.

How to reach Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple and Hayagrib Cave

You can reach the place by Sakhu-Sali Nadi Temple- Nagarkot Road or Bhaktapur-Telkot-Nagarkot Road.

By Sakhu-Sali Nadi Temple- Nagarkot Road

Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple and Hayagrib Cave are at a distance of 15 km from Chabahil, Kathmandu.

By Public Vehicle

You can get a public vehicle from Chabahil, Kathmandu to Sali Nadi Temple. It may take around one hour to reach Sali Nadi Temple. From there, you need to walk for about 30 minutes to reach the destination.

By Private Vehicle

You need to take the right from Sakhu to reach the destination. It may take about one hour to reach the destination if you are in your private vehicle from Chabahil, Kathmandu.

By Bhaktapur-Telkot-Nagarkot Road

Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple and Hayagrib Cave are at a distance of 9 km from Nagarkot Bus Stop, Kamalbinayak, Bhaktapur.

By Public Vehicle

You can get a public vehicle from Nagarkot Bus Stop to Telkot. Telkot is 7 km ahead of Kamalbinayak. It may take about 30 minutes to reach Telkot from Nagarkot Bus Stop, Kamalbinayak. From there, you need to walk for about 15 minutes to reach the destination.

By Private Vehicle

You need to take the left from Telkot to reach the destination. It may take about 30 minutes to reach the destination if you are in your private vehicle.
