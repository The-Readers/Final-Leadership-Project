Pancha Pokhari is a beautiful place that lies in the Himalayas of Sindhupalchok District. Located at an altitude of approximately 4100 meters from sea level, this is a holy place for Hindu people. The meaning of the name behind Panch Pokhari is in the Nepali language “Panch” means “Five” and “Pokhari” means “Lake”. Thus, the meaning of Panch Pokhari is a hill of five lakes. This is because you can find five ponds at the hilltop in the same place. Similarly, this place is famous during the famous Hindu festival, Rishi Panchami or Janai Purnima. Thousands of pilgrims from inside and outside the country visit this holy place during the festival.


Panch Pokhari lies in Panchpokhari Thangpal Rural Municipality of Sindhupalchok District, Nepal. This holy place is under the Langtang National Park. Likewise, this place is also famous for short treks near the Kathmandu Valley. The place at the top is well facilitated with many hotels to stay overnight. Similarly, the trekking route is also well maintained. The route takes you through dense green forests and beautiful hills. The warm hospitality of local people on the trekking route is the never-forgetting moment you will ever experience in your life.


The upper places in the route of the Panch Pokhari trek pass through dense Rhododendron forests. Meanwhile, the view of forests during the Falgun-Baisakh (February-April) is breathtaking. You will not regret a single second while visiting the holy Panch Pokhari. Similarly, the views of the mountains in the north are breathtaking. On a clear day, you can see the mesmerizing view of the Jugal Himal. Moreover, this region offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and trekking adventure.

History of Panch Pokhari

The history of Panch Pokhari dates back to about 2400 years ago. There was a hunter called Bumba Ruwa Waiba. One day he went hunting and reached Panch Pokhari. Meanwhile, at the same time, he saw Lord Shiva planting paddy in the snowy field. Waiba was shocked when he found someone planting paddy in the snowy field. After that, Waiba went near the Lord Shiva and when he was about to reach the place where he was planting paddy, Lord Shiva suddenly disappeared. Lord Shiva disappeared exactly at the place where today lies the famous temple.
Still, there is a belief that some people see the “Halo”(plough) and “Juwa”(beam) that is used to plough or dig the field below the temple. Meanwhile, there is an outlet of the pond as a waterfall. Some people believe that they can see the Halo and Juwa near the waterfall. And, Hunter Waiba believed that he saw Lord Shiva and he did not have anything to offer. He had a hunter dog and there was a bell on the neck of the dog. He offered the bell at the temple and this can be still seen by the visitors and pilgrims who visit the temple.

Religious importance of Panch Pokhari

Pilgrims visiting Panch Pokhari Temple believe this place has a great value in the Hindu community. There is a huge fair during the Rishi Panchami or Janai Purnima. According to the local people this year, 2023 more than 8,000 people visited during this festival. Meanwhile, the hotels and lodges remain packed during this event. Even visitors sleep in the “Goth” (Cow Shelter) during the festival time. Likewise, there is a tap to take a holy bath below the famous temple area. During the Janai Purnima, pilgrims take the holy bath and change their “Janai” during this festival.
Secondly, there is a belief that the people who do not have children are blessed to have the child after visiting and worshipping in this holy temple. Thus, the Hindus and Buddhists people who do not have children visit, offer prayers, and take blessings from the temple.
There are five ponds within the Panch Pokhari area. They are called as Suryakunda, Naagkunda, Saraswatikunda, Ganeshkunda, and Bhairabkunda.
