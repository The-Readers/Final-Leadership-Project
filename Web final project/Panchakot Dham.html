Establishment of Panchakot Dham

Panchakot Dham lies Panchakot Ekikrit Basti in Ward-6 of Baglung Municipality. Since the establishment of this temple complex, this area has been one of the popular tourist destinations in the Baglung district. This place is popular all over Nepal. The infrastructure of this temple complex was built under the leadership of the famous Muktinath Baba. He is the same person who is behind the building of the famous Shree Pancha Mahalaxmi Temple in Bhaktapur. Moreover, Muktinath Baba was born in Panchakot near the Panchakot Dham complex. Swami Kamalnayan Acharya Shri Ji Maharaj (Muktinath Baba) had a dream of building a pilgrimage site in his hometown. After the construction of this temple complex, his dream changed into reality. Panchakot Dham consists of five different kots (temples) in the same place.

The five different kots consist of Tatajalkot, Karikot, Majhkot, Raynaraynakot, and Sansaarkot. These five different places are rich in history. Ancient kings used to visit these places to worship and seek blessings. The main motive for building this temple complex is Muktinath Baba wanted to build a pilgrimage site of all gods and goddesses in the same place. So, after the construction of this Panchakot Dham, pilgrims can worship different gods and goddesses around the temple complex at the same place. Rishi Prapannacharya stated that this temple is for all communities of Nepalese people. They did not just want to establish this place as a religious place. Further, they wanted this place as a unique place all over the country. The construction of a park and a big statue of Lord Hanuman is beautiful to watch. Likewise, the construction of the temple took place in the year 2014.

Architecture and Features


Panchakot Dham at the top of Baglung is a beautiful temple complex. Similarly, the architecture of the temple is in the traditional Nepali style of architecture as well as the South Indian Structure of Temples. The temple complex has several temples that include the main temple, which is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is beautified with carvings and sculptures. Likewise, we can find interior decoration with colorful murals and paintings. In addition to the main temple, there are several smaller temples that are dedicated to other Hindu deities. Other Hindu deities are Lord Ganesha, Lord Krishna and Radha, and Goddess Durga. There is a garden at the top of the temple complex. At the end of the garden, there is an eye-catching big statue of Lord Hanuman holding a Sumeru Parbat in his left hand.

The design of the temple complex is in such a way that it creates a peaceful atmosphere. Tourists come here not only to visit but to worship and meditate also. The complex lies at the hilltop above Baglung Bazaar amongst dense green forests and hills. The architecture and style of the temple complex add beauty to the place. Similarly, the architecture makes it a popular tourist destination for religious significance as well. There is a belief that visiting the temple complex and offering prayers can bring blessings and good fortune. According to the locals, you can find tigers in the temple complex sometimes.

Places to visit in Panchakot Dham

Panchakot Dham is not a single temple. It is a temple complex with many temples and other monuments around the temple area. If you are visiting this place, you will get lost in nature and the beauty of the temple area. You can book hotels at Panchakot Dham from hotels.com. The places to visit in the Panchakot Dham area are as below:

Panchakot Garden

It is a garden above the Panchakot Dham complex. It is on the way to the temples below while visiting the temple complex from above. Likewise, there are different varieties of flowers and ornamental plants planted in the garden. From the end of the garden, you can see the panoramic view of the Himalayan Ranges. Also, you can see the landscape view of Baglung Bazaar below from the same place. You can also enjoy the swing at the end of the garden facing the smiling mountains.

Panchakot Garden

Lord Hanuman Statue

The large statue of Lord Hanuman lies at the end of the Panchakot Garden along with the swing. Likewise, the statue is big and surrounded by fences and railings. It is the statue where Lord Hanuman is carrying Sumeru Parbat in his left hand and Gada in his right hand. During the snowfall or on a clear day you can see the large statue of Lord Hanuman in front of eye-catching mountain ranges.

Lord Hanuman Statue at Panchakot

Water Pot (Gagri) Statue

Just below the statue of Lord Hanuman and Panchakot Garden, there is a Gagri statue. It is large in size and painted in a color like copper. It reflects the traditional water pitcher used for storing and carrying water.

Gagri

Sheesh Mahal

There is a temple below the Panchakot Garden. The interior of the temple is decorated beautifully with glasses and mirrors. Because of this reason, the temple is called Sheesh Mahal. Similarly, inside the temple, there are statues of Lord Krishna and Radha. You need to pay for the ticket to enter the Sheesh Mahal. It is one of the main attractions of this temple complex.

Sheesh Mahal

Ganesh Temple

It is the temple above the Garud Monument. It is the first temple because, in Hindu Mythology, Shree Ganesh means to start. Similarly, it is a beautiful temple with the architecture of the South Indian Temple style.

Shree Ganesh Temple

Lord Vishnu and Garud Monument

Lord Vishnu and Garud Monument lie just beside the Sheesh Mahal. You can see the beautiful sculpture of the eagle and the statue of Lord Vishnu together. Moreover, the sculpture of an eagle is one of the eye-catching and most popular architectures to experience in the Panchakot temple complex.

Lord Vishnu and Garud

The Cave

There is a cave below the sculpture of the eagle. Inside the cave, there are statues of Tigers. According to the locals, there used to live a tiger in the cave. Moreover, inside the cave, there are statues of Lord Shiva, Rishi, and a snake. The cave is well-lightened and remains bright anytime.

Why is Panchakot Dham famous?

Panchakot Dham is famous for its unique religious structures, scenic location, and growing reputation as a spiritual and cultural hub in Nepal.

Who made Panchakot Dham?

Panchakot Dham was not made by a single person but is the result of a collective effort. It was conceived and developed through community contributions, donations, and the support of local religious leaders like Muktinath Baba.
