Sali Nadi Temple is a famous Swasthani Mata Temple that lies in Sakhu. This temple area is the origin of the famous Manohara River of Kathmandu that afterward mixes with the holy Bagmati River. Likewise, this place lies in Shankharapur Municipality in the Kathmandu district of Nepal. The name of the place is called Sankharapur because the shape of the town is similar to Sankha. About Famous Swasthani Brata Katha

Swasthani Brata is a Hindu religious ritual and a sacred story read during the month of Magh (January-February) by Nepalese Hindus. Swasthani Brata is a famous prayer in the name of Swasthani Mata. During this event, the devotees read about the story of Swasthani Mata and take fasting for a whole month. The famous Swasthani Brata Katha is a story about Swasthani Mata and the devotes perform this ritual in order to honor Goddess Swasthani. Similarly, Hindu devotees believe in Goddess Swasthani as a goddess who possesses the power to grant wishes and provide blessings to her devotees. During this ritual, the devotees narrate or read the Swasthani Brata Katha in the morning and in the evening. The reading of the Swasthani Brata Katha is for spiritual growth and enlightenment to the devotees.

The Swasthani Brata Katha is a collection of stories that describes the power and grace of Goddess Swasthani. Similarly, the stories are narrated in epic form. Hindu people believe the story is based on Skanda Purana. The ritual of the Swasthani Brata Katha lasts for a month that falls during the Nepali month Poush-Magh. The famous Swasthani Brata ritual begins on the full moon day of the Nepali month of Magh and ends on the full moon day of the following month. And there is a belief among the devotees that reading this story can fulfill their wishes and gain spiritual growth. Similarly, during this ritual the devotees offer prayers, fast, meditate, and perform various religious rituals. The devotees also visit different temples dedicated to Swasthani Mata. Moreover, there is also a belief that by performing this ritual, one can attain peace, prosperity, and happiness in life.

History

The Sali Nadi Temple has a fascinating story that dates back a few centuries ago. According to the local people, during the Malla Dynasty, the temple was made. The Malla Dynasty ruled over Kathmandu Valley from the 12th to 18th century. Likewise, the Malla Kings were famous for the arts and culture. Sakhu was a separate kingdom before the Malla Dynasty in Nepal. According to a legend, Bajrayogini instructed the priest Jogdev and the king Sankhadev. The instruction was to build the town Sakhu in the shape of a shell (Sankha). Similarly, the Sali Nadi Temple was built by Pratap Malla during the Malla dynasty in the year 1655.

Architecture of Sali Nadi Temple

The main temple of at Sali Nadi complex is a famous eight-cornered temple at the bank of Sali Nadi. This temple is famous for the name Swasthani Mata Mandir. The temple has a gold-plated roof on top and is supported by wooden architecture that features the carvings of various gods and goddesses. Inside the temple, there is a big statue of the goddess seating on a lion. We can see fascinating wood carvings in the windows and doors of the temple. Moreover, inside the temple, you can see the walls painted with scenes from Hindu mythology. The temple complex is paved with bricks and has a large bell on the gate of the temple.

We can see the Sali Nadi Temple built in the traditional Newari style of architecture. This temple complex is famous for its complicated wood carvings and decorations around the temple. The main temple of Sali Nadi is dedicated to Lord Shiva. In the temple, devotees worship Lord Shiva in the form of a lingam. Similarly, the temple complex has other shrines dedicated to other Hindu deities such as Lord Ganesha, Goddess Saraswati, and Goddess Kali.

Significance

The Sali Nadi Temple has great cultural and religious significance for the people of Nepal, especially the Hindu people. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, Lord Shiva is one of the primary gods of the Hindu religion. Likewise, devotees from inside and outside the country come to the temple to offer prayers and seek blessings from Lord Shiva. The temple lies on the bank of Sali Nadi Temple which is significant to watch. The river is a holy river and the origin of the famous Manohara River. There is a belief among the pilgrims that taking a dip in the river can purify one’s sins. As a result, many devotees come to the temple to offer prayers and especially take a dip in the river below.


There is a festival during Shrawan Sombar, which is also a significant cultural event in Nepal. Likewise, this festival is celebrated among Hindus during the month of Shrawan (July-August) in the Nepali calendar dedicating Lord Shiva. During this festival, devotees take fasting, come to the temple to offer prayers, perform rituals, and seek the blessings of Lord Shiva.

How to reach Sali Nadi Temple

Sali Nadi Temple lies at a distance of approximately 14 km from Chabahil, Kathmandu. You can get a public vehicle from Chabahil to the destination or you can book a cab as well.
